efile Coach - Faqs

BEFORE STARTING

Companies: The client company details. By default, the system creates a company called 'Private Client', which you should assign to all those coachees who hire you directly.

Individuals: The details of those clients you do the sessions with.

Processses (Company Processes and Individual Processes): The details agreed on the contract for the Coaching process. Number of sessions, meeting place, price, etc. They may either be Company Processes, which are those where a company hires you to do sessions with its employees (your coachees), or Individual Processes, which are those where the coachee hires you directly.

When you register a coachee, you will have to select who you are signing the agreement of the process with. It may either be with a company or the coachee him/herself. You will also need to assign a Coach to the process. You will not have access to the sessions or the tools unless you have completed this step.

This is set up in this way because the application is prepared for companies that may hire several Coaches, assigning them to different processes while safeguarding the confidentiality of the client company details. This also allows the Coaches to keep the confidentiaity of their sessions.

Similarly, there is a sample contract at your disposal under the modules of 'Companies' and 'Individuals'. The Confidentiality Agreement is in a separate module, 'Coaching Tools', because its administation is under the responsibility of the Coach, especially so when the Admin user has several Coaches registered on the application.

Sessions: Details about the content of each Coaching session with a coachee.

GENERAL

Which are the first steps to start using efile Coach?

The first thing to know that you may find useful is that the application is divided into two distinct areas. On the one hand, there is the free section for client information management. Modules: 'Companies' and 'Individuals'. On the other hand, there is the section for the administration of coaching processes and their sessions. Modules: 'Processes' and 'Sessions' (free during the first 14 days, and paid from the 15th day onward).

The first step to start using the application is to enter your Coachee's information. Keep in mind that some contracts, reports, etc. automatically load the details from these fields; therefore, if all of them are not filled out, these documents will appear incomplete.

The second step is to complete the details of the process by assigning a coach to the client and by specifying who signs the Coaching agreement. This may either be the client him/herself or, in case of a company process, the company for which this person works.

The third step is to transcribe the sessions that you do in the 'Sessions' module. You will find this very useful when it is time to prepare the future sessions. Depending on your client, you may also send him/her the session reports by choosing the sections that you wish to share with him/her.

CONTRACTS & REPORTS

When do I send the contract? When do I sign it?

Usually, prior to starting the sessions, your clients will have already gotten in touch with you to dispel any doubts about whether Coaching will be helpful for them. Most likely, you will have talked about what is and what is not Coaching, and will have discussed if Coaching is what they need according to the urgency, the initial objective, etc. At the time when you agree to start a Coaching process, set a number of sessions, set the price, etc., you may also ask for their details to prepare the contract, etc., and let them know that you will send them the contract along with the confidentiality agreement and the code of ethics. Once you have your clients' details, you may register them on the application and send them the documents for their review prior to their signature.

You may arrange a meeting before the session to sign the documents. If you have already agreed on the conditions and you have sent them the contract for review, you may want to sign it just before the first session, or even use the signature on the contact as your alliance with the client.

Is a client's email reply with an 'OK' to the contract or the confidentiality agreement legally valid?

The documents are sent as attached files in an email; and therefore, an 'OK' in an email would not constitute a valid means to prove that it is referring to the content of the attached file (contract, etc.). Consequently, it is convenient to print them out for their signature next time you meet your clients in person. If they agree, there should be no problems, and you may spare yourself a disappointment in the future.

Do you have tripartite agreements?

In Spain, as well as in many other countries, it is illegal for a contract signed by two sides to involve a third party. In a Coaching process, in a contract with a company where there are details that the coachees are not expected to know, and the responsibilities only concern the two sides (Coach and Company), it is not legal for coachees to sign because they do not hold any responsibility over these points.

In order to comply with the requirements of a Tripartite Alliance, you have the following documents at your disposal: 'Agreement with Coachee' and 'Confidentiality Agreement'.

What is the Initial Survey for?

The Initial Survey is a document intended to gather some information when you have not had the opportunity to meet with your clients, and you wish to know how they heard about you or what they know about Coaching. It is useful to know what is working well in your sales strategy by collecting this information, helping you to manage your clients' expectations before the first interview. If you have had the opportunity to speak to your coachees in person, you may have already been able to collect this information and may not consider it necessary to send them the survey.

I cannot select any coachee in the 'Work Tools' module. What can I do?

Check that you have completed filling out all the process details and that you have assigned your client a Coach. Only those Coaches that have been assigned to clients have access to their respective information.

COACHING TOOLS

Can I print a copy of the SWOT Analysis?

The SWOT Analysis is a very useful document to provide the coachees with a heightened awareness, and it is commonly used in companies. In the case of a client wishing to do a SWOT Analysis, you may send him/her an email where he/she will fill out the SWOT details on-line. When finished, you will receive an alert so that you can acces 'Tools', select your coachee again, and print out a copy of the SWOT Analysis that will appear on a new 'See SWOT' button. You will be able to bring it with you to the next session to work with it with your client.

Can I choose the areas to be evaluated by the 360-Degree Feedback?

This option is currently unavailable. We have created this 360-Degree Feedback version with the general and widely used competencies in the corporate world, divided according to the critieria of the IE Business School.

What is the maximum number of evaluators that I can include in the 360-Degree Feedback?

There is no limit. The tool compares your coachee's perception with the average of all the received evaluations. Moreover, we suggest it be used with no less than 8 evaluators to generate reliable data.
